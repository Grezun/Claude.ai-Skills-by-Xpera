# Corporate Legal Playbook — Standard Redline Language

Reference for `legal-contract-redliner-xpera`. Use this language when drafting redline proposals.

---

## Indemnification

### Mutual Indemnification (standard)
> Each party ("Indemnitor") shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party and its officers, directors, employees, and agents ("Indemnitees") from and against any third-party claims, losses, damages, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from the Indemnitor's (a) breach of this Agreement, (b) negligence or willful misconduct, or (c) violation of applicable law.

### Indemnification Cap
> Notwithstanding the foregoing, each party's total indemnification obligations under this Section shall not exceed the greater of (i) the fees paid or payable by Customer to Vendor in the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the claim giving rise to the obligation, or (ii) [USD $X].

### IP Infringement Indemnity (vendor obligation)
> Vendor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Customer from and against any third-party claims alleging that the Services, as provided by Vendor and used by Customer in accordance with this Agreement, infringe or misappropriate any patent, copyright, trademark, or trade secret of a third party.

## Limitation of Liability

### Mutual LoL (standard)
> EXCEPT FOR (A) EITHER PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTION [X], (B) EITHER PARTY'S BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS, (C) FRAUD OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (D) AMOUNTS OWED FOR SERVICES DELIVERED, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, REGARDLESS OF THE FORM OF ACTION AND WHETHER OR NOT SUCH DAMAGES WERE FORESEEABLE.

### Mutual Aggregate Cap
> EXCEPT AS SET FORTH ABOVE, EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE GREATER OF (A) THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE CLAIM, OR (B) [USD $XX,XXX].

## Auto-Renewal

### Standard Auto-Renewal with Adequate Notice Window
> This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods equal to the then-current term (or one (1) year, whichever is shorter) unless either party provides the other with written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

## IP Ownership

### Work-for-Hire with Pre-Existing IP Carve-Out (services/consulting)
> All work product, deliverables, and materials specifically created by [Contractor] for [Company] under a Statement of Work ("Work Product") shall be considered works made for hire owned exclusively by [Company]. To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as a work made for hire, [Contractor] hereby irrevocably assigns all right, title, and interest therein to [Company].

> [Contractor] retains all right, title, and interest in (a) any pre-existing intellectual property owned by [Contractor] prior to the Effective Date ("Pre-Existing IP"), (b) general tools, methodologies, frameworks, and know-how developed independently of this Agreement, and (c) any improvements to [Contractor]'s Pre-Existing IP, even if incorporated into Work Product. [Contractor] grants [Company] a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use any Pre-Existing IP incorporated into Work Product solely to the extent necessary to use the Work Product.

### SaaS — Customer Data Ownership
> As between the parties, Customer retains all right, title, and interest in and to Customer Data. Vendor shall not use Customer Data for any purpose other than to provide the Services under this Agreement. Vendor shall not sell, transfer, or disclose Customer Data to any third party except as required by applicable law or as authorized in writing by Customer.

## Non-Compete / Non-Solicitation

### Balanced Non-Compete (if required)
> During the term of this Agreement and for a period of six (6) months following its termination or expiration, [Party] shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in any business that directly competes with [specific defined business activity] in [defined geographic area].

### Employee Non-Solicitation (mutual)
> During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months thereafter, neither party shall actively solicit for employment any employee of the other party who was directly involved in the performance of this Agreement, without the prior written consent of the employing party. This restriction shall not apply to (a) general public job postings not targeting the other party's employees, or (b) employees who have been separated from the employing party for at least three (3) months.

## Confidentiality

### Bounded Definition of Confidential Information
> "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one party ("Discloser") to the other ("Recipient") that is designated in writing as "Confidential" or "Proprietary" at the time of disclosure, or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

### Standard Exclusions
> Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by Recipient; (b) was rightfully known to Recipient prior to disclosure without restriction; (c) is rightfully received by Recipient from a third party without restriction; or (d) is independently developed by Recipient without use of or reference to Discloser's Confidential Information.

### Confidentiality Duration
> The obligations of this Section shall survive for three (3) years following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations with respect to information that constitutes a trade secret under applicable law shall survive for as long as such information remains a trade secret.

## Termination

### Termination for Convenience
> Either party may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason upon sixty (60) days' written notice to the other party.

### Termination for Cause with Cure Period
> Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the other party specifying the breach in reasonable detail, if the breaching party fails to cure such breach within such thirty (30)-day period (or, if such breach is incapable of being cured within thirty (30) days, if the breaching party fails to commence a cure and diligently pursue it to completion within sixty (60) days).

### Data Return on Termination
> Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Vendor shall, at Customer's election and expense, (a) return all Customer Data in a machine-readable format within thirty (30) days of Customer's written request, or (b) certify in writing that all Customer Data has been securely deleted or destroyed. Vendor shall maintain Customer Data in an accessible format for no less than thirty (30) days following termination to allow Customer to retrieve it.

---

## Assignment

### Consent Required with M&A Exception
> Neither party may assign this Agreement or any rights hereunder, or delegate any obligations hereunder, without the prior written consent of the other party, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either party may assign this Agreement without consent (a) to an affiliate, or (b) in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets to which this Agreement relates, provided the assignee assumes all obligations hereunder in writing. Any purported assignment in violation of this Section shall be void.

## SLA / Service Levels

### Termination Right for Repeated SLA Failure
> In addition to any service credits set forth in Exhibit [X], if Vendor fails to meet the applicable SLA for three (3) or more consecutive months, or for four (4) or more months in any rolling twelve (12)-month period, Customer shall have the right to terminate this Agreement for cause upon thirty (30) days' written notice without any early termination penalty.

---

## Governing Law / Dispute Resolution

### Mutual Right to Injunctive Relief
> Notwithstanding any obligation to arbitrate disputes hereunder, either party may seek emergency or interim injunctive relief from a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm, without the requirement to post a bond or other security and without waiving the right to arbitrate the underlying dispute.

## Price Escalation

### Annual Increase Cap
> Vendor may increase fees upon renewal by providing at least sixty (60) days' prior written notice; provided that any such increase shall not exceed the lesser of (a) [three percent (3%)] or (b) the percentage change in the Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U) for the preceding twelve (12) months as published by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.
